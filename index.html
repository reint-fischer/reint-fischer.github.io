Welcome

I am a Graduate Student at the Department of Atmospheric and Oceanic Science at the University of Maryland. I am fascinated by geophysical fluid dynamics, from large scale currents to mesoscale and submesoscale eddies and instabilities to small scale waves and 3D turbulence. I started my adventure in research studying Lagrangian Ocean Dynamics at Utrecht University. On this page I share my research projects, including some personal interests.

The Bering Sea

For my work as a Graduate student at the University of Maryland, I am looking at waves, storms and sea ice in the Bering Sea

Lagrangian Ocean Dynamics

At Utrecht University I used OceanParcels to study the dynamics of microplastics and how algae growing on their surfaces might affect them. We wrote this paper about it: Fischer, R., Lobelle, D., Kooi, M., Koelmans, A., Onink, V., Laufkötter, C., Amaral-Zettler, L., Yool, A., and van Sebille, E.: Modelling submerged biofouled microplastics and their vertical trajectories, Biogeosciences, 19, 2211–2234, https://doi.org/10.5194/bg-19-2211-2022, 2022.

Using color in visualization

I think mapping and visualizing atmosphere and ocean variables is an important and very fun way to build intuition of concepts that are hard to understand and see with the naked eye. I wrote this notebook to discuss the use of color in maps, to show how to make the most of it and how to avoid misleading your audience.

Sports analytics
